Common Tomato Diseases

1.Bacterial Spot

Symptoms

Small, water-soaked spots on leaves, stems, and fruit that turn brown and scabby

Causes:

Caused by bacteria from the genus Xanthomonas (e.g., Xanthomonas vesicatoria).

2.Early Blight

Symptoms:

Dark brown spots with yellow halos on older leaves, which can lead to leaf drop

Causes:

Caused by the fungus Alternaria solani.

3.Late Blight

Symptoms:

Large, dark brown blotches with a fuzzy white mold on leaves and stems

Causes:

Caused by the water mold Phytophthora infestans.

4.Leaf Mold

Symptoms:

Yellow spots appear,Olive-green to brown mold forms.

Causes:

Caused by the fungus Cladosporium fulvum

5.Septoria_leaf_spot

Symptoms:


Leaf Spots: Small, round spots with dark brown margins on the lower leaves.
Yellowing Leaves: Infected leaves turn yellow, dry up, and fall off.

Causes:


Fungus: Caused by the fungus Septoria lycopersici
Environmental Conditions: Thrives in warm, humid, and wet conditions

6.Spider_mites Two-spotted_spider_mite

Symptoms


Leaf Discoloration: Yellow, white, or brown stippling on leaves.
Webbing: Fine webbing on the underside of leaves and sometimes covering the plant.

Causes


Warm, Dry Conditions: Ideal environment for spider mites to thrive.
Excess Nitrogen: Over-fertilization can promote mite infestations.

7.Target_Spot

Symptoms:


Leaf Spots: Small, dark brown spots with concentric rings (target-like appearance) on leaves
Premature Leaf Drop: Infected leaves may wither and fall off the plant.

Causes:


Fungus: Caused by the fungal pathogen Corynespora cassiicola.
Environmental Conditions: Thrives in warm, humid, and wet conditions.

8.Tomato_mosaic_virus

Symptoms:


Foliage: Mottled leaves with yellow and dark green areas.
Fruit: Yellow blotches, necrotic spots, and internal browning.

Causes:


Virus: Caused by the Tomato Mosaic Virus (ToMV).
Transmission: Spread through infected seeds, tools, and human handling.

9.Tomato_Yellow_Leaf_Curl_Virus

Symptoms:


Leaf Curling: Leaves curl upwards and become crumpled.
Yellowing: Leaves show interveinal yellowing and stunted growth

Causes:


Virus: Caused by the Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV).
Vector: Spread by the silverleaf whitefly (Bemisia tabaci)
